Instruction

For this assignment, you will choose one of the two digital collections of The Tower. One is The Tower Archive (from 1922 to 2018, https://cuislandora.wrlc.org/islandora/object/cuislandora%3A67501) and the other is The Tower (from 2019 to 2022, http://cuatower.com/). The Tower Archive is a part of the Catholic University Archives (https://libraries.catholic.edu/special-collections/archives/index.html). The Tower has served as the student newspaper at The Catholic University of America since the Fall of 1922. For this assignment, you will analyze the system and discuss how you can improve the user interface to the collection and redesign it to better facilitate information retrieval, based on the heuristics we have been discussing, the readings, and the digital archives you have explored. You will also conduct a "mini-usability test" to observe how a real person experiences the site. You will be analyzing the site from the perspective of supporting undergraduate teachers who want to incorporate original source material into their lesson plans and student learning activities.

 

This assignment is broken into two suggested parts.

 

PART 1 - Analyze the website using the usability framework.

 

Step 1 - Explore and consider the information that goes into (input) or comes out (output) of the system. List 4 inputs and their corresponding outputs of this system. Present your results as a table of inputs and outputs. The input in an information system can be any action that initiates an interaction. The output of the system is the types of information provided as the results of the interaction. Identify each type of information presented as outcome, such as title, images, page number, etc.

 

Step 2 - In this step you will critique the site from the perspective of supporting undergraduate teachers who want to incorporate original source material into their lesson plans and student learning activities. Use the Nielsen’s heuristics to guide your analysis.

 

Consider the goals of the teachers and their students. One of the teacher's goals is to find specific material relevant to a topic (e.g., changing attitudes toward gender, race, politics, etc.) to incorporate into a lesson. A student might similarly be assigned to find material about that topic.

Pick a topic (some ideas are: tobacco use, armed conflict, or changing attitudes toward gender and race) and look for material that illustrate aspects of the topic. Use the site for about 20 minutes. As you explore, take notes of the strengths and weaknesses of the site (you don't need to turn in your notes).

 

Step 3 - Summarize the PART 1 with the top 2 strengths and top 2 weaknesses of the system. Explain your assessment. Provide a few screenshots as appropriate to illustrate your summary.

 

PART 2 - Conduct a “mini-usability test”

In this part of the assignment, you will observe how an end-user uses the site for a similar goal.

 

Step 4 - Find one friend or colleague who is willing to spend 20 minutes using the site. Ask them to do the following two tasks:

Task 1 - Use the site to explore how the CUA student newspaper has changed over the years.

Spend up to 5 minutes on this.

 

Task 2 - Imagine that you are a teacher looking for material about campus perspectives on World War II and the Vietnam War. Find two articles about each conflict that effectively illustrate some aspect about campus attitudes and activities. Spend up to 10 minutes on this.

 

While they are doing these tasks, observe what they do. Do they seem excited and interested, engaged, satisfied, or frustrated? What problems do they run into? What goes smoothly? You may ask them brief questions while they are doing the tasks, but don't interrupt them for more than 30 seconds or so. Take notes on the observation form I provide. Afterwards, ask them to complete the brief post-questionnaire.

 

Step 5 - Briefly compare the results from PARTs 1 and 2 (1-2 paragraphs). What similarities and difference did you see between your heuristic analysis and the mini-usability test? Include some suggestions for improvement based on your findings.
